# Round 15 — GTM & Narrative Design Doc

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"The TechCrunch Doom Cycle."** Player gets featured on TechCrunch (dopamine spike, customers flood in), then immediately gets hit with a support ticket avalanche or server outage. This one-two punch is the screenshot moment — players will share "I was rich for exactly one day" stories. The events system turns every run into a war story worth telling.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **Day 5-10:** First random event fires. Tone is scrappy — office lease hikes, a viral tweet from a nobody account. Stakes are small but feel enormous at $50k runway.
- **Day 15-25:** TechCrunch feature lands. The setup line should read like a push notification that stops your heart. Immediate follow-up events (churn spike, competitor launch) teach players that growth without infrastructure is a trap.
- **Milestone beats:** MVP ships with a "it's ugly but it works" celebration. Beta gets "first real customer who isn't your mom." V1.0 is a genuine triumph moment. Scale is quiet confidence — "the machine runs itself now."

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

He stays silent. But after the TechCrunch event fires, his sprite should be facing his laptop instead of staring into space. After a bad event (server outage, poaching), he's briefly facing the player — like he noticed. No dialog. No explanation. Just enough to make players post "what is he DOING" in threads.

## 4. Signature Dialog

**Event: Viral Tweet**
> *"@CloudDad69 just called your product 'the only SaaS that doesn't make me want to unsubscribe from life.' It has 4,200 retweets."*
>
> **A)** Ride the wave — retweet from company account. *(+8 customers, 20% chance of attracting a troll that costs -$3k in PR damage)*
> **B)** Stay quiet and let it breathe. *(+3 customers, no risk)*

## 5. Startup Tropes & GTM Mechanics

- **VC Coffee Meeting:** Classic "we're not investing but let's pick your brain for an hour" — choice between burning half a day for a maybe-intro or politely declining.
- **Competitor Launch:** A YC company ships your exact feature. Do you pivot messaging or double down? This mirrors real positioning decisions.
- **Feature Request Avalanche:** Enterprise prospect wants 12 features. Build custom and lock in revenue, or protect the roadmap? This is the real SaaS trap — services revenue disguised as product-market fit.
- **Regulatory Scare:** GDPR-flavored. Pay for compliance now or gamble that nobody checks. The fine scales with customer count — punishes growth-at-all-costs players.

Every event reinforces the core GTM truth: **growth without resilience is just a more expensive way to die.**
