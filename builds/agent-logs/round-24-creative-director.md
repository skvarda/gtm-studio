### Core Viral Hook
The status screen *is* the joke. Every founder compulsively checks Stripe and Baremetrics at 2am — now players do it in a 16-bit office with a menu that opens like a Pokemon trainer card. The shareable moment: screenshot the panel when runway hits 4 days, post it. The red cash warning does the emotional work. Misery is content.

---

### Key Narrative Beats

**Beat 1 — The First Check-In.** First time the menu opens, a one-liner flashes below the panel: *"Data is not strategy. But it's a start."*

**Beat 2 — The Red Zone.** When runway < 15 days, the panel border pulses red. No dialog. Just the color. The game doesn't lecture — it shows.

**Beat 3 — Exit to Title Confirm.** The prompt reads: *"Shut it down? This can't be undone."* Startup shutdown language embedded in the UI layer.

---

### Co-Founder Evolution (Subtle)
Zero changes mechanically. But when the player opens the status screen with no customers and a negative MRR trajectory, the co-founder's sprite turns *one frame* toward the player. No dialog. He noticed. He's still not saying anything.

---

### The Signature Dialog

> **[Status Menu, runway = 8 days]**
> `Cash: $3,200 | Burn: $400/day | Runway: 8 days`
> *(small text, PAL.hudYellow)*
> *"Your Series A deck says 18 months of runway."*

---

### GTM Mechanics to Wire In
- **Vanity vs. signal**: MRR displayed above customer count — trains the player to care about revenue quality, not headcount.
- **Dashboard refresh compulsion**: Opening the menu is free. Mirrors founder behavior — information is free, decisions are expensive.
- **Runway in days, not months**: This is the only clock that actually creates urgency.
