# GTM Studio — Narrative & Dialog Doc, Round 29
**Codename:** "What's Your Name, Founder?"

---

## Key Story Beats

**Beat 1 — The Blank Slate**
The cursor blinks. The hoodie sprite stands still. This is the only moment in the game where nothing is on fire yet. Name yourself. Step in.

**Beat 2 — First Day Arrives**
After name entry, the office fades in. The welcome dialog fires immediately — personal, dry, charged:

> *"Welcome to the office, {name}. Your angel funding just hit the account. $120K to change the world... or at least ship v1."*

No fanfare. Just a number and a deadline. That's startup life.

---

## NPC Dialog — The Co-Founder

He's already at his desk when you arrive. He doesn't look up.

> *"Oh. You made it."*
> *(beat)*
> *"Coffee's cold. Whiteboard's yours."*

No name. No handshake. Exactly as intended.

- **Name entry prompt:** `ENTER YOUR NAME, FOUNDER`

- **Empty name fallback toast:** `"Alex" it is. Classic.`
- **HUD label:** `{name} — Day {n}`
- **Day-end summary header:** `END OF DAY {n}, {name}`

---

## Event Notifications

- First hire (dev): *"{name} posted the job at 11pm. Someone actually applied."*
- First customer: *"A stranger on the internet is now paying {name} $49/month. Wild."*
- Cash warning: *"Runway: 30 days. {name}, this would be a great time to have a plan."*
- Game over: *"The dream is over, {name}. But the whiteboard is still there."*

---

## Tone Guidelines

- Dry over dramatic. The humor is in the understatement.
- The co-founder never explains himself. That's the joke. That's also the mystery.
- {name} makes every line feel earned — use it sparingly enough that it still lands.
