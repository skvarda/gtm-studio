# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 8

## 1. The Core Viral Hook

**"Your first startup, speedrun."** The moment players watch their cash tick down $2,740/day while agonizing over Build vs. Sell — that's the screenshot. The tension of three action points per day, visible burn rate, and a game-over clock creates the exact anxiety-comedy loop that drives Twitter clips. Players will post their Day 47 bankruptcies and Day 12 lucky MRR spikes. The HUD *is* the hook — real numbers, real dread, real dopamine.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats This Round

- **Day 1**: The terminal boots. "You have $150,000. The clock is ticking." Typed out letter by letter. The game just became real.
- **First Build action**: Optimistic energy. "You shipped a login page. It only takes passwords longer than 32 characters. Product progress: 8%."
- **First failed Sales Outreach**: "You cold-emailed a VP of Engineering. He replied 'unsubscribe.' Respect the hustle."
- **Day ~20 cash anxiety**: The HUD cash counter turns yellow below $80K, red below $40K. No dialog needed — the color does the storytelling.
- **Game Over**: Not dramatic. Quiet. "You ran out of money on Day 43. The office Wi-Fi was shut off first."

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

Keep him ambient. This round, he occasionally glances at the player sprite when they choose "Sales Outreach" — a single head-turn animation, two frames. No dialog about it. If the player interacts with him, he says one of three rotating lines, all deflections: "Just thinking." / "Don't worry about it." / "I have a call later." Plant the seed that he's tracking your decisions. Nothing more.

## 4. Signature Dialog

> Terminal action — Build Product (outcome #3):
> "You refactored the authentication module for the fourth time. It's mass beautiful. No one will ever see it. Product progress: +4%."

## 5. Startup Tropes as Mechanics

- **Premature optimization trap**: Building too much product without sales burns cash with no MRR to show. The math punishes "just one more feature" thinking — mirroring real founder mistakes.
- **Cold outreach RNG**: Sales actions have a ~30% hit rate early, teaching players that pipeline is a numbers game. Failed attempts still generate flavor text, keeping rejection entertaining.
- **Burn rate as game clock**: Not abstract. $2,740/day is derived from real seed-stage math (2 founders × $500K/yr loaded cost). Players who've lived this will feel it in their chest.
