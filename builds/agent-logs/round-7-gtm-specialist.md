# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 7

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"What does your co-founder actually do?"** This is the water-cooler question. Every startup has one person whose contribution is... unclear. We lean into this hard. Screenshots of the co-founder sitting at his desk doing nothing while you grind will be instantly relatable to anyone who's worked at a startup. The hook isn't the gameplay — it's the shared trauma. Twitter/Reddit posts write themselves: "This game gave me a co-founder who just sits there and I've never felt more seen."

## 2. Key Narrative Beats — Round 7

- **Beat 1: The Empty Office.** Player spawns into a small, quiet office. Two desks, one door, a terminal. The silence says everything — you're pre-revenue, pre-team, pre-everything.
- **Beat 2: The Presence.** The co-founder is already there. He doesn't greet you. He's just... at his desk. Monitor glow on his face. No explanation offered.
- **Beat 3: The Terminal.** Walking to the computer establishes it as the future interaction point — where you'll build product, check metrics, hire. For now, it just hums.

## 3. Co-Founder NPC Evolution (Subtle)

This round: purely ambient. He sits. Occasionally shifts in his chair (idle animation, single frame swap). No dialogue trigger, no interaction prompt. The mystery is built by *absence* of information. Later rounds will layer in micro-details — a second monitor appears, sticky notes accumulate, he's occasionally not at his desk. Players should start asking questions the game never explicitly answers.

## 4. Signature Dialog Line

When the player first approaches the terminal:

> `> BOOT SEQUENCE... OK`
> `> RUNWAY: 287 DAYS`
> `> TODO: EVERYTHING`

This sets tone — dry, self-aware, slightly ominous. The terminal talks like a founder's internal monologue.

## 5. Startup Tropes & GTM Mechanics to Incorporate

- **Runway as health bar** — every real founder watches this number. It creates inherent tension without enemies.
- **Build vs. sell tension** — the first real decision (hire dev or salesperson) mirrors actual Series A debates.
- **"Stealth mode"** — the empty office *is* stealth mode. No customers, no product, just potential and burn rate.
- **Investor update anxiety** — future mechanic, but plant the seed: the terminal will eventually ping you with "INVESTOR UPDATE DUE."
