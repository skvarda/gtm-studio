# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 20

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"Four ways to win, all of them feel like losing."** The endgame paths mirror real startup outcomes — IPO sounds glamorous until you realize you need 200 customers and a full team; Profit Machine means you played it safe; R&D Utopia means you're a lab not a business; World Domination means you ground yourself down. Players will screenshot their victory screens and argue about which path is "real" success. That debate *is* the marketing.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **Day ~15-20**: First random event fires. The game stops feeling like a sandbox and starts feeling like survival. "A competitor launched" should hit early and hard.
- **Product v1.0 Launch**: The emotional peak of mid-game. You've been grinding AP into a progress bar for days — the launch fanfare should feel *earned*. Post-launch, the terminal action swap ("Scale Product") signals the game has shifted under you.
- **Victory approach**: When a player crosses 75% on any path, a subtle HUD pulse draws attention. No fanfare yet — just dread and excitement.

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

He still does nothing obvious. But starting this round, random events occasionally reference him obliquely: "Your co-founder was seen talking to someone from Sequoia in the parking lot." No mechanical impact. No dialog tree. Just... atmospheric. Players should start theorizing. Plant exactly enough to fuel speculation, not enough to confirm anything.

## 4. Signature Dialog

**[Random Event — "Key Employee Wants a Raise"]**

> **Alex (Dev):** "Hey, so… I got a LinkedIn message. From Stripe."
> *[Long pause. Pixel sweat drop.]*
> "I'm not saying I'm leaving. I'm saying my mass transit commute costs have increased."
>
> **→ [Give raise: +$2K/mo burn]**
> **→ [Inspirational speech: 40% chance they leave anyway]**

## 5. Startup Tropes to Incorporate

- **Series A as a Faustian bargain** — the VC event should feel tempting and ominous simultaneously. Cash influx, but burn jumps and a "Board Meeting" random event enters the pool.
- **Hacker News spike** — the viral blog post event should grant customers *only* if product quality is high enough. Premature virality kills startups. Teach that.
- **Server outage at the worst time** — always fires when MRR is highest. Because of course it does.
