# Playtesting Report — Round 11
**SaaS Startup Simulator | First Playable Assessment**

---

## 1. What Works Well (Conceptually)

The premise is strong. "Harvest Moon but you're burning angel funding" is an immediately understood pitch. The tension between building product and chasing revenue is a natural strategic fork that mirrors real startup pain. The mysterious co-founder is a great slow-burn narrative hook — players will speculate about him constantly. The 16-bit SNES aesthetic is smart scoping; it keeps art costs sane and leans into nostalgia without competing with polished modern sims. Four distinct endgame paths give genuine replayability. The tone — absurdist startup humor with real strategic weight underneath — is a rare combination that could land hard if executed right.

## 2. Top 3 Anticipated Issues

**Bug #1 — The First Hire Decision Feels Blind.** Developer vs. salesperson is the defining early fork, but without visible feedback loops (burn rate projections, revenue forecasts), players will feel punished by a coin flip rather than a strategy. Needs lightweight UI signals before commitment.

**Bug #2 — Cash Burn Pacing Will Be Off On First Pass.** Either the runway is too generous (no tension) or too punishing (restart loop frustration). This number needs aggressive tuning with multiple playthroughs. Expect it to be wrong initially.

**Bug #3 — The Co-Founder Risks Feeling Like a Placeholder.** If he just stands in the office doing nothing for 80% of the game with no micro-interactions, players will forget he exists before the late-game reveal matters. He needs ambient behavior — cryptic one-liners, occasional disappearances, subtle foreshadowing.

## 3. Feel Assessment

**Uncertain.** The concept has warmth, but the first playable must prove that the office space feels *alive* — NPCs moving, ambient sounds, monitor glow. Without that, it's a spreadsheet with pixels.

## 4. Pacing Assessment

**High risk.** The early game carries the entire onboarding burden. If the first 10 minutes don't clearly communicate "you are hemorrhaging money and must act," the strategic depth never surfaces. Front-load urgency.

## 5. One Improvement That Would Make the Most Difference

**Ship a visible, ticking cash-burn counter on the HUD from frame one.** Nothing else communicates the core loop faster. Every decision the player makes should visibly move that number. It's the heartbeat of the entire game. Build everything else around it.
