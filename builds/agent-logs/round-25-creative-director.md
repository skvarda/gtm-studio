### Highlights:

**Viral hook:** The dev/salesperson asymmetry is a two-line screenshot that sells itself — *"Lost my train of thought. Thanks."* next to *"I'M ON FIRE."* Every founder has lived it.

**Co-founder beat:** No dialog. No productivity change. Just a pixel chair rotation on the third interaction. The player notices. Nothing is explained. The mystery deepens through absence while everyone else gains a voice.

**Signature line:**
> *"I had the bug. I had it. It was right there."*

That's the dev on interaction 3. It's both funny and genuinely painful — captures the interruption cost better than any tooltip could.

**GTM angle:** The mechanic maps 1:1 to real startup pathology — async interruption tax for makers, relationship momentum for sellers. The ping pong table as a usable but purposeless object doubles as commentary on startup vanity metrics. *"You serve an ace to nobody. Startup life."*
