# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 18

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"The ending screen is a screenshot machine."** Victory overlays with tinted pixel art, a stat-box revealing your run's story (days survived, hires made, cash burned), and the co-founder finally standing up — this is the moment players screenshot and share. Each of the four paths produces a distinct visual: gold shimmer for IPO, green for Profit Machine, blue for R&D Utopia, red for World Domination. The implicit challenge: "Which ending did YOU get?" drives replay and social posting. Save/load means players can actually chase all four without losing progress.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **Victory as punchline.** Each ending reframes the entire run through a different lens. You didn't just accumulate numbers — you became a specific kind of founder.
- **The save icon flash** is a tiny narrative beat: the game acknowledging it remembers you. "Progress saved." feels weirdly emotional after 30 days of burn rate anxiety.
- **Day 15 threshold.** The purple monitor triggers only after you've survived long enough to stop panicking. It rewards patience with weirdness.

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

He does nothing new mechanically. That's the point. But at victory, he *stands up* — the only time his sprite changes in the entire game. No dialog from him. No explanation. He just rises from his chair as the overlay fades in. Players will project meaning onto this. Let them. The purple monitor easter egg is the one crack in his opacity: evidence he's playing a game inside the game. Plant the seed. Don't water it yet.

## 4. Signature Dialog

**Purple monitor interaction:**
> "You glance at the screen. It's... a spreadsheet? No, wait — it's a game. Inside a game. You feel briefly uncertain about the nature of your Series A."

## 5. Startup Tropes to Incorporate

- **Victory stat: "Mass Emails Sent: 0"** — a dig at founders who think code ships itself.
- **IPO ending text:** "The bankers arrive. They're wearing nicer hoodies than you."
- **Profit Machine ending:** "You did it. Sustainable unit economics. Your Twitter following drops by half."

These tropes ground the endings in real founder absurdity — recognizable enough to share, specific enough to feel earned.
