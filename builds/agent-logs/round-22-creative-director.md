# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 22

---

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"The only startup sim where time feels real."**

The pacing fix IS the marketing. When players tweet "I spent 20 minutes just deciding whether to hire sales or dev," that's earned media. The emotional hook: the dread of a burning runway that you *feel* in real time, not just watch tick down. Tagline candidate: *"Burn rate. Real rate."*

---

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **Day 1:** Angel wire hits. The office is empty except you and the co-founder. First action feels weighty — hire someone, or ship something?
- **Day 7:** First customer churns. No warning. Dialog from a sales hire: *"They said the product felt unfinished."* Player must decide: panic-hire a dev, or double down on revenue?
- **Day 14:** Market shift event. A competitor ships. Co-founder looks up from his monitor for exactly one frame. Then back down.
- **Endgame trigger:** Runway hits 30 days. The office goes quiet. Idle animations slow. The weight lands.

---

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution (Subtle)

No new dialog. No confrontation. Instead: his idle animation changes. Before Day 10, he types. After Day 10, he *reads* — something off-screen. After a market shift event, there's a sticky note on his monitor that wasn't there before. Players who look closely will notice. No one explains it.

---

## 4. Sample Dialog

> **[SALES HIRE — Day 4, after first cold call]**
> "Got a reply. They want a demo next Tuesday."
> *(pause)*
> "...What does our product actually do again?"

---

## 5. GTM Mechanics to Incorporate

- **ICP targeting:** Early customers define your "lane" — a soft reputation system that gates later customer types
- **Churn as feedback loop:** Churned customers leave a one-line reason; player can spend AP to "read the exit survey"
- **Viral coefficient:** Every 3rd customer acquired through sales can optionally refer one more — free, but random timing
