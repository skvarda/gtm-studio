# Round 27 Narrative & Dialog Doc — "Sound & Scanlines"

---

## Key Story Beats

**The Office Comes Alive.** On first keypress, the title theme kicks in — your co-founder glances up from his monitor for exactly one frame, then looks back down. He noticed. He won't say anything.

**The Mute Toggle.** Press M. The co-founder's head tilts slightly. *Did he just... react to silence?*

---

## NPC Dialog — The Co-Founder

> *(on hire event, cash register SFX plays)*
> "Good hire. Probably."

> *(on game over, descending tone)*
> "I had a backup plan. I just didn't tell you."

> *(on victory fanfare)*
> "I always believed in us." *(beat)* "Mostly."

> *(idle, rarely)*
> "Do you hear that? ...Never mind."

---

## UI Text & Flavor

- **Mute ON**: `♪ — (silence is also a strategy)`
- **Mute OFF**: `♪ — (the dream is loud again)`
- **AP depleted**: `No bandwidth. Literally.`
- **Day End jingle tooltip**: `Another day survived. The soundtrack agrees.`
- **Hire confirmation**: `Offer letter sent. Someone just changed their LinkedIn.`
- **Action denied**: `You can't do everything. That's what VCs don't understand.`

---

## Event Descriptions

- **New customer acquired**: *Cha-ching. One less month until ramen profitability.*
- **Feature shipped**: *It works on your machine. Ship it.*
- **Runway critical**: *The music gets quieter when you're losing. That's intentional.*

---

## Tone Guidelines

Write like a founder who's read too many Paul Graham essays and not enough therapy books. Humor lives in the gap between ambition and reality. The co-founder speaks rarely — when he does, it lands. SFX and music are *diegetic to the fiction*: the office hears them too. Lean into that.

---

*Every sound tells the story. The scanlines just make it look right.*
