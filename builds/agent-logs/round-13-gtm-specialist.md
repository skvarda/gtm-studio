# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 13

## 1. The Core Viral Hook

**"Screenshot the moment you went Default Alive."** Ramen Profitability is the most emotionally resonant milestone in startup life. Every founder remembers it. When that sticky note appears on the office wall and the lights brighten, players will screenshot it. The customer board is the second hook — watching tiny logos accumulate (and vanish in red) is the kind of ambient anxiety that makes people say "you have to see this." Design the board so it looks good in a crop.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **First Dollar**: Understated. A single line: the co-founder glances up from his laptop, nods once, looks back down. That's it. The restraint makes it land.
- **Ramen Profitable**: The big emotional beat. Dialog box, lights shift, sticky note. The player earned this.
- **PMF Signal**: Tone shifts from survival to ambition. The VC email arrives. The game is now about what kind of company you *choose* to build.
- **Morale crisis**: First time an employee threatens to quit, the player realizes people aren't just stat blocks. This is the mid-game's teeth.

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

He still doesn't explain himself. But at Ramen Profitable, the player notices his screen briefly — it shows a spreadsheet with numbers that don't match yours. At PMF Signal, he takes a phone call and steps off-screen for one day-tick. No explanation. Seed, don't resolve.

## 5. Startup Tropes & Real GTM Mechanics

- **Logo board as social proof**: Mirrors the real practice of putting customer logos on your landing page. More logos = morale boost + investor interest. Meta-referential.
- **Churn as visible loss**: SaaS founders obsess over churn dashboards. Making churn *visual* (red flash, logo disappears) teaches the player that retention > acquisition without a tutorial.
- **The poaching event**: "A FAANG recruiter DMed your best engineer." Real, constant, painful. Forces a morale investment decision.
- **Team pizza as band-aid**: Every startup has done the "morale is low, order pizza" move. Make it work slightly — but not enough if the underlying problem persists. Diminishing returns on repeat use.
