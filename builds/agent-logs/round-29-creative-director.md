# GTM Studio — Narrative & GTM Design Doc, Round 29
**Codename:** "What's Your Name, Founder?"

---

## 1. Core Viral Hook

Identity. The moment a player types their own name and sees it surface in dialog — *"Welcome to the office, Jamie. $120K to change the world."* — the fourth wall cracks in the best way. The game stops being a simulator and starts being *their* startup. That's the screenshot moment. That's the tweet.

---

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **Beat 1 — The Blank Slate:** Name entry screen. Cursor blinks. The hoodie sprite waits. The player names themselves and steps into the fiction.
- **Beat 2 — The Welcome:** First dialog lands with the player's name. Angel money is in the account. The world is wide open.
- **Beat 3 — First Day Friction:** AP system makes the scarcity real. Do you hire or build? The name in the HUD is a constant reminder: *you* made this call.

---

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution (Subtle)

He notices the name entry. One line, day one, unprompted: *"Hm. {name}. Good name for a founder."* Then nothing. He returns to his monitor. No explanation. The player will think about it for three days.

---

## 4. Signature Dialog

> *"Welcome to the office, {name}. Your angel funding just hit the account. $120K to change the world... or at least ship v1. The coffee machine is broken. Priorities."*

Warm. Self-aware. Sets tone in four sentences.

---

## 5. GTM Mechanics & Tropes

- **Named founder = accountability loop.** The HUD name makes losses feel personal, wins feel earned.
- **"Founder-led sales"** trope: early customers only trust *you* by name. Sales NPC unlocks scale, but the first customer should reference the founder's name in their dialog.
- Incorporate the classic **"first customer is always a friend"** beat — grounding the abstraction in recognizable startup reality.
