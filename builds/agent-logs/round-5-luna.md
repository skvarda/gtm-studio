# Creative Brief: Day 1 Content (Round 22)

**For:** Sage (Writer) & Felix (Game Designer)  
**Round Priority:** day1_content  
**Target Beat:** Character Creation & Apartment Day 1

## Tone Direction

**Cozy Morning Energy** — This is the first day of everything. The player just quit their job, has angel funding burning a hole in their pocket, and is sitting in a cramped apartment with their co-founder. Tone should feel like:

- **Excited nervousness** — "Holy shit, we're really doing this"
- **Warm but slightly awkward** — Jordan and player know each other from a hackathon but aren't best friends yet
- **Grounded optimism** — Big dreams, small apartment, real work ahead
- **Subtle world-building** — Hints about the broader startup ecosystem without overwhelming the intimate apartment setting

Think: First day of school meets first day at a new job meets "we're about to change the world (maybe)."

## NPCs Speaking This Round

**Jordan (Primary Focus)**
- Currently at apartment position (8,3), facing down, typing with occasional pauses
- Needs 3 dialogue variants based on player's role selection (seller/dev/marketer)
- Jordan's mysterious nature should be present but subtle — they're helpful but there's something they're not saying
- Should feel like a real co-founder relationship: supportive, complementary, slightly distant

## Emotional Beat Target

**"The Beginning of Something"** — By the end of this content, the player should feel:
1. **Oriented** — Clear on their role, the apartment space, and immediate goals
2. **Connected** — Jordan feels like a real person, not just an info-dump NPC
3. **Motivated** — Ready to start building, with a sense of possibility
4. **Grounded** — This is hard work, but it's *their* work

## Must Include

### Core Character Creation
- Role selection dialogue that feels natural (not menu-driven)
- Jordan's response should reflect the player's choice meaningfully
- Each role path should hint at different gameplay styles ahead

### Apartment Exploration Beats
- Laptop interaction — where the magic happens
- Whiteboard interaction — planning and dreaming space
- Natural conversation with Jordan that establishes their relationship dynamic
- Apartment should feel lived-in and authentic to two broke twenty-somethings

### Foundation Laying
- Establish the "one week = one day" time scale naturally
- Introduce Action Point concept organically (not as a game mechanic explanation)
- Set up the daily rhythm: morning check-ins, afternoon work, evening reflection

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- **Other locations** — Only apartment content this round
- **External emails** — Focus on interpersonal apartment content first
- **Complex game systems** — Keep mechanics introduction light and natural
- **Other NPCs** — Jordan is the only confirmed character for dialogue
- **Business strategy details** — Save deep SaaS mechanics for after character creation is complete

## Key Constraints

- Jordan is the only implemented NPC available for dialogue
- All interactions must occur in the apartment location
- Player role is currently unset — this content should establish it
- Day 1, garage era — everything should feel scrappy and intimate

## Success Metrics

Player should finish this content thinking:
- "I understand who I am in this world"
- "Jordan is interesting and I want to learn more about them"  
- "I'm ready to start building something"
- "This apartment feels like home base"

**Delivery Focus:** Character depth over system complexity. Make Jordan memorable.
