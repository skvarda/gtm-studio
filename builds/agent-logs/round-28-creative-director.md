# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 28: "First Light"

**By: Creative Director | SaaS Startup Simulator**

---

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"Your startup finally has a face."** Round 28 is the moment the world stops looking like colored rectangles and starts looking like *people*. The hook is simple and primal — players see Jordan rendered as a real sprite for the first time and feel the game snap from prototype to product. That transition mirrors the startup experience itself: the first time something you built looks real. Share-worthy screenshot moment, zero extra effort.

---

## 2. Key Narrative Beats

- **"Loading..." screen** doubles as in-universe flavor — the company is "booting up" each morning, coffee not yet brewed.
- Jordan's idle sprite appears in the corner of the office for the first time as a real character, not a placeholder. No dialog. No fanfare. He's just *there*, staring at his second monitor.
- The floor tiles gain warmth — the office starts feeling like a place someone might actually want to work. Cozy, not corporate.

---

## 3. Mystery Co-Founder Evolution

Jordan gets a face this round, but no voice. That's intentional. The sprite renders and he does exactly what he always does: nothing visible. Let players project onto him. A glasses-wearing, hoodie-clad figure in the corner is a Rorschach test — some will find him comforting, some unsettling. Don't explain him. Not yet.

*Subtle tell for attentive players:* Jordan's idle animation (if any) never cycles to "looking at the player." He's always facing slightly away. Always busy with something off-screen.

---

## 4. Signature Dialog

> **[Day 3 — You walk past Jordan's desk]**
> *Jordan doesn't look up.*
> **Jordan:** "Preloading."
> *...He means his coffee.*

---

## 5. GTM Mechanics & Tropes

- **Art pipeline as product launch** — the asset preloader mirrors a real SaaS deploy pipeline: manifest, load, verify, fallback. Teach players systems thinking through the UI.
- **Graceful degradation as startup resilience** — the fallback renderer is a startup metaphor. Ship with what you have. Degrade gracefully. Never go dark.
- **"Works on my machine"** — Ren's QA pass on scaling/alignment is the classic pre-demo panic. Surface this as flavor text on load.
