# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 11

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"The Day You Didn't See Coming."** Every day-end event is a screenshot moment. Players will share the absurd scenarios — "My co-founder was spotted at a competitor's office on Day 7 and I only have $800 left" — because startup trauma is universally relatable content. The binary/triple choices create natural debate ("you should have taken the angel money"), driving engagement loops on social. The hook isn't the simulator. It's the stories it generates.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats This Round

- **Days 1-5**: Events skew environmental — server hiccups, a Hacker News comment, a cold email from a "growth hacker." Teaches the system without killing the player.
- **Days 6-12**: Stakes escalate. Employee poaching, customer churn waves, a surprisingly aggressive enterprise prospect who wants a feature you haven't built. The player feels the burn rate as a living threat.
- **Days 13+**: Milestone-gated events unlock. An acquisition whisper. A VC slide deck request. The co-founder starts acting strange. The game shifts from survival to trajectory.

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution

He stays peripheral. But his dialogue now reflects *your* state with unsettling accuracy. Low cash: he's "reading a book about stoicism." New hire: "Interesting choice." Hit a milestone: he nods but says nothing for a full beat before speaking. He never congratulates directly. One line at MRR $500: *"Not bad. I've seen faster, but not bad."* The player should start wondering what he knows.

## 4. Signature Dialog

Event: **"Viral Tweet"**
> *"Someone with 50K followers called your product 'the ugly duckling of SaaS.' MRR spiked. Your co-founder looks up from his laptop and says: 'All press is press.' He goes back to whatever he was doing."*

## 5. Startup Tropes & Real GTM Mechanics

- **Churn as weather**: MRR loss events mirror real logo churn — normalizes it as manageable, not fatal.
- **Premature scaling trap**: Hiring events tempt you to grow before product-market fit. The game punishes this exactly like reality does.
- **Angel strings**: Cash injection events with conditions (give up AP next turn, lock a feature choice) mirror real term sheet tradeoffs.
- **Milestone unlocks as PMF signals**: Crossing MRR $500 or Product 50% gates new event tiers, mirroring how real startups unlock distribution channels at inflection points.
