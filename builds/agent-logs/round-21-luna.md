# Creative Brief: SaaS Quest Days 2-3 Content
**Round Priority:** days_2_3_content  
**Target:** Story content for game days 2-3  
**Audience:** Sage (Writer), Felix (Game Designer)

---

## TONE DIRECTION

**Days 2-3 Emotional Arc:** "The Honeymoon Ends"

Day 1 was all possibility and nervous energy. Days 2-3 should feel like **reality setting in**. The initial excitement is wearing off, and the weight of what they've committed to is becoming real. Think: the third day of a new job when the training wheels come off.

**Tone Palette:**
- **Warm but with edges** — Still cozy pixel art vibes, but introduce subtle anxiety
- **Jordan gets slightly more cryptic** — Less obvious support, more mysterious observations  
- **Money pressure creeps in** — Not panic yet, but the burn rate math starts mattering
- **Chad escalates** — From friendly competitor to low-key threat

**Reference Points:**
- Silicon Valley (HBO): Episode 2-3 energy when the stakes become real
- Stardew Valley: When you realize winter is coming and you're not prepared
- Earthbound: When the adventure stops feeling like a game

---

## NPC FOCUS

**Jordan (Primary Focus)**
- Location: Apartment only
- Character development: Begin revealing the mystery arc
- Dialogue should become **10% more cryptic, 20% less obviously supportive**
- Start dropping hints about Jordan's true agenda/background
- Still helpful, but with an undercurrent of "there's something you don't know"

**No other NPCs available this round** — all character interaction happens through Jordan, laptop interactions, and environmental storytelling.

---

## EMOTIONAL BEAT TARGETS

**Day 2 Target:** "Wait, this is actually hard"
- Reality check moment
- First glimpse of Jordan's mystery
- Chad becomes a real presence (not just one email)

**Day 3 Target:** "The money is real and finite" 
- Burn rate anxiety kicks in
- Jordan's support becomes more conditional/cryptic
- First hint that not everyone wants them to succeed

**Overall Arc:** Transition from "we can do anything!" to "oh, we actually have to do this specific thing well or we're screwed."

---

## MUST INCLUDE

### Jordan Dialogue (Days 2-3)
- **2-3 conversations per day** (triggered by walking past, direct interaction)
- **Mystery arc progression:** Jordan should say something that makes the player think "wait, what did they mean by that?"
- **Burn rate anxiety:** Jordan should mention money/runway in a way that makes it feel real
- **Co-founder dynamic:** Still supportive but less obviously so

### Chad Email Escalation
- **Day 2:** Follow-up that's slightly more aggressive/competitive
- **Day 3:** Something that feels like intelligence gathering or subtle undermining
- **Maintain plausible deniability** — Chad should always sound "helpful" on the surface

### Burn Rate Anxiety Flavor Text
- **Laptop interactions:** Dashboard should start showing burn rate calculations
- **Environmental details:** Things that make the $150K feel finite
- **Atmospheric tension:** Subtle signs that money matters

### Gazette Issue 2
- **Broader startup ecosystem stories** that affect player psychology
- **Competitive landscape hints** — other companies succeeding/failing
- **Economic context** that makes funding feel precious

---

## EXPLICITLY OUT OF SCOPE

- **No new NPCs** — Jordan only, apartment only
- **No new locations** — All action happens in apartment
- **No hiring yet** — Too early for employee interactions  
- **No customer acquisition** — Still pre-product/pre-market
- **No major plot progression** — This is character/tension building, not resolution
- **No Jordan secret reveals** — Build mystery, don't resolve it

---

## SUCCESS METRICS

Content succeeds if:
1. Player feels **slightly more anxious** about money by end of Day 3
2. Jordan feels **10% more mysterious** without losing supportiveness  
3. Chad feels like a **real competitive threat**, not just background noise
4. Player wants to know **what Jordan isn't telling them**
5. The apartment starts feeling **smaller/more constraining**

**Failure state:** If player still feels like "everything's fine, we have tons of time" by end of Day 3, we haven't built enough tension.

---

## TECHNICAL CONSTRAINTS

- **Single location:** All content must work within apartment layout
- **Jordan positioning:** Currently at (8,3) facing down, typing behavior
- **Dialogue system:** Support for player response choices with relationship effects
- **Email system:** Established sender format (Chad, sender_company, etc.)
- **Gazette format:** Digest style with headline/summary structure
