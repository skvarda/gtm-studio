## KEY STORY BEATS

**Day 1 — The Wire Hits**
Angel money clears overnight. The office smells like new carpet and ambition. Your co-founder is already at his desk, doing... something. First decision: hire or build?

*Notification:* `$500k received. Runway: ~18 months. Clock starts now.`

**Day 7 — First Signal**
A customer replies to your cold email. They're interested, not buying. The co-founder glances up from his monitor, says nothing, goes back to typing.

**Day 14 — The Pivot Temptation**
A competitor launches something adjacent. Product feels half-done. Sales feels impossible. This is the first real test.

**Day 30 — Inflection**
Either customers are growing or cash is collapsing. There is no middle. The co-founder gets up, makes coffee, and leaves a sticky note on your monitor.

## TONE GUIDELINES

Write like a founder who's been there: **wry, not cynical. Urgent, not panicked.** The absurdity is real — a sticky note that changes everything, a co-founder who speaks in three words. Humor lives in specificity. Don't say "things are bad." Say "the runway counter just went red and the office AC kicked on simultaneously."

Every line should feel like it costs something to say.
