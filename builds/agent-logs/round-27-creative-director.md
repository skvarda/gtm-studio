# Round 27 GTM & Narrative Design Doc — "Sound & Scanlines"

---

## Core Viral Hook

**"The startup that sounds like your childhood."**

The moment audio hits, this game stops being a browser toy and becomes a memory. The hook is sensory nostalgia colliding with Silicon Valley absurdity — SNES chiptunes playing while you agonize over whether to hire sales or engineering first. Screenshot-worthy, sharable, instantly legible: *it's Stardew Valley but you're burning VC money.*

---

## Key Narrative Beats

- **Day 1 audio drop**: First keypress triggers the title theme. Players feel the tonal shift immediately — this isn't a demo anymore.
- **The Grind Loop**: Gameplay BGM reinforces the "one more day" compulsion. Lo-fi + chiptune = psychological comfort during hard decisions.
- **Day-end stinger**: A 2-second jingle punctuates every transition. Small ritual, big conditioning. Players start anticipating it.
- **First hire moment**: The cash register SFX on hiring lands as comedy and reward simultaneously. It's the game winking at you.

---

## Co-Founder Evolution (Subtle)

When the player is muted (M key toggled), the co-founder's idle sprite gains a faint animation — headphones bobbing slightly. He was listening the whole time. No dialog. No acknowledgment. Just a tell. Players who notice it will screenshot it.

---

## Startup Tropes & GTM Mechanics

- **Audio as product signal**: Mute toggle doubles as a "focus mode" metaphor — ship in silence, celebrate in sound.
- **Sensory feedback loop**: SFX on denied actions (no AP buzz) trains players without tutorial text. Show, don't tell.
- **CRT filter = perceived polish**: Players conflate visual craft with game depth. Retention goes up when it *looks* intentional.
- **Jingle as retention hook**: Pavlovian day-end loop mirrors real product notification design — you built the habit system into the game itself.
