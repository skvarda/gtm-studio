# Round 23 GTM & Narrative Design Doc
## "Make It Feel Alive"

---

### Core Viral Hook

**"The startup sim that respects your time — and makes you feel every wasted hour."**

The hook isn't grinding for stats. It's *atmosphere*. Players screenshot their office at 11pm (Evening phase, monitors flickering orange) and post it. The world feels like a real dysfunctional startup, not a spreadsheet. Organic shareability comes from the *vibe*, not leaderboards.

---

### Key Narrative Beats This Round

- **Morning Phase (AP 3→2):** Office is bright, energy is high. Sprites are upright, monitors cool blue. Player feels optimistic.
- **Afternoon Phase (AP 2→1):** The grind sets in. Window tint warms. The dev employee starts fidgeting — shoulder rolls, head dips.
- **Evening Phase (AP 1→0):** Monitors cast amber light. The co-founder's typing slows to single keystrokes. Silence has weight. The player chooses when to go home.

---

### The Mystery Co-Founder: Subtle Evolution

He types all day. Every day. Nobody knows what he's building. This round: add a **2-frame "pause and stare at screen"** idle animation that triggers randomly at Evening phase only. No dialog. No explanation. Players will notice. Players will speculate. That's the whole point.

---

### Signature Dialog (captures the tone)

> **[New hire, Day 3, after first customer acquired]**
> *"So... do we have product-market fit yet?"*
> **[You]:** "We have one customer and a Notion doc. So yes."

---

### GTM Mechanics & Tropes to Incorporate

- **"Founder Mode"** buff: End the day at full 3 AP unspent — sacrifice short-term for a next-day morale bonus. Classic hustle mythology, gently mocked.
- **Cosmetic social proof:** Floating `+1 Customer` text styled like a Stripe notification. Founders are trained to dopamine-loop on this.
- Real GTM beat: **time-of-day friction**. Morning actions yield slightly more product progress. Evening sales calls convert worse. Subtle, never explained — players discover it.
