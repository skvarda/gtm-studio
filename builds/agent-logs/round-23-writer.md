Here's a summary of the creative choices:

**Tone anchor:** Deadpan competence. The humor lives in specificity ("47 tickets labeled CRITICAL") not slapstick. Every line sounds overheard, then slightly exaggerated.

**Co-founder voice:** The defining creative decision here — he speaks in fragments, never explains, never reassures. "Still here." could be solidarity or observation. Players will debate it. That ambiguity is the mystery arc doing its job without a single dramatic scene.

**Three-phase narrative arc:** Each phase has a one-line flavor text that tracks psychological deterioration across the day — possibility (Morning) → cognitive overload (Afternoon) → dissociation (Evening). The orange monitor glow at Evening is the emotional climax of every single day.

**AP feedback copy:** Each action line is self-aware about startup futility ("They'll say they need to loop in procurement") without being cynical. The player feels seen, not mocked.

**UI tooltip on AP:** "Time is the only non-renewable resource. (Also cash.)" — does double duty as game mechanic explanation and thematic statement.
