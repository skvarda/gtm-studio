# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 6

## 1. Core Viral Hook

**"What does your co-founder actually do?"** — This is the shareability engine. Players will screenshot his cryptic lines, theorize on forums, and argue about whether he's a genius or a fraud. The mystery creates organic word-of-mouth. Secondary hook: the brutal cash-burn timer creates "I survived 847 days" bragging moments — shareable failure and triumph states, just like roguelikes.

## 2. Key Narrative Beats (Round 6)

- **Day 1**: "You have $500K and a dream. Your co-founder is already here. He nods." — sets tone immediately.
- **First Hire**: "They show up Monday. They ask about equity. You change the subject." — humor through avoidance.
- **First Customer** ($49 MRR): "Someone is paying for this. On purpose." — disbelief as comedy.
- **Cash Below $100K**: "The runway is getting short. Your co-founder seems unbothered." — tension plus mystery.
- **Game Over**: "Your startup ran out of runway. Your co-founder has already updated his LinkedIn."

## 3. Co-Founder Evolution (Subtle)

Round 6 keeps him static but seeds future payoff. His lines should be 80% meaningless, 20% accidentally profound. He never reacts to your decisions. He's always already at the office when you arrive. One detail: his monitor faces away from you. Always. Players who pay attention will notice. Later rounds reveal whether he's a visionary, a con artist, or something stranger.

## 4. Signature Dialog Line

> **You interact with the co-founder.**
> He doesn't look up.
> *"I moved us to a different cloud region. Don't check which one."*

## 5. Startup Tropes as Mechanics

- **Hire devs or salespeople first** mirrors the real build-vs-sell founder debate
- **Burn rate anxiety** is every seed-stage founder's lived experience
- **Product quality gating customers** reflects actual product-market fit mechanics
- MRR as the core score metric teaches real SaaS literacy

The game teaches GTM by making you live it.
