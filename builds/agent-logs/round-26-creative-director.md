# GTM & Narrative Design Doc — Round 26

## Core Viral Hook

The opening sequence *is* the pitch. Players name their startup, get immediately patronized by their own co-founder, then choose a class that determines how the rest of the game punishes them. The hook: **Darren exists**. Screenshot-worthy, quotable, and instantly relatable to anyone who has ever worked with a guy like Darren. Social share vector is the class selection screen — players post their pick and argue about it.

## Key Narrative Beats

1. **Name your company** — Stakes feel real immediately. You're not playing a game, you're founding something.
2. **Darren introduces himself** — He's already comfortable. He's been here. He has opinions about the office layout.
3. **"So what is it you do again?"** — The pivot. Darren doesn't ask this to be cruel. He genuinely isn't sure. This is his brand.
4. **Class selection** — Player defines themselves in response to Darren's question. The game's first power dynamic: Darren prompted it, you answer.

## Darren's Evolution (Subtle)

Darren should do *slightly* more than nothing, but never admit it. Round 26: he asks one question. Round 27: he's on a call when you arrive. Round 28: there's a whiteboard with his handwriting. The mystery compounds. Players should begin to theorize. Never confirm.

## Signature Dialog

> **Darren:** "I was thinking — and hear me out — what if we didn't launch until it was ready?"
> *(beat)*
> **Darren:** "Anyway. What is it you do again?"

Tone: confident, unhelpful, weirdly charming.

## GTM Mechanics to Incorporate

- **Founder-market fit** baked into class selection — Seller opens sales calls early, Dev unlocks a stealth feature sprint, Marketer gets a free PR event in Week 2.
- **"Founder story"** stat hinted at but not yet visible — set the seed now.
- Class choice visible to Darren: he has one unique reaction line per class. Barely acknowledges it. That's the joke.
