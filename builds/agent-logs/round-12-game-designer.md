**Key design decisions:**

- **Events don't start until Day 5** — gives players time to learn the base loop before introducing variance. No surprise deaths on Day 2.
- **MVP is a hard gate on growth** — forces the "hire dev or salesperson first?" decision to have real teeth. Sales-first players get early trickle revenue but stall without product. Dev-first players burn cash longer but unlock the growth engine sooner.
- **Every event is a two-choice tradeoff, never a pure penalty** — keeps player agency front and center. Even bad events feel like "I chose wrong" not "the game screwed me."
- **The TechCrunch Moment** is the engineered fun beat — a guaranteed high-low emotional sequence that mimics real startup whiplash. It's also inherently shareable, which ties back to the viral hook strategy.

The difficulty curve has a critical split around Day 13. Players who shipped MVP start compounding. Players who didn't are in a death spiral. That's where the game reveals its strategic depth.
